The Internet’s Big Missing Feature is Annotation

The Internet’s Big Missing Feature is Annotation

Only a handful of people know that the big missing feature from the web browser – the feature that was supposed to be in from the start but didn’t make it – is the ability to annotate any page on the Internet with commentary and additional information... I often wonder how the Internet would have turned out differently if users had been able to annotate everything – to add new layers of knowledge to all knowledge, on and on, ad infinitum.

Marc Andreeseen
CEO, Andreeseen Horowitz

The creator of the first web browser Mosaic - Marc Andreeseen - says the Internet has a Big Missing Feature - Annotation! The Webpage was always meant to be a Launchpad for Insight and Debate on it’s contents. Annotations were removed from the Mosaic browser in 1995 during the fight for survival with Microsoft Explorer for lack of storage - there was no Cloud!

Today’s web has evolved to be mostly Walled Gardens with limited Interactions and few external links, which opens the door for things we don’t like. On the macro-level, as outlined in Zucked https://zuckedbook.com, the business models and algorithms of the Internet Platforms control choices and modify behavior, leading to serious problems such as fake news, election hacking, and addiction/depression. On the micro-level, you may have noticed too many tabs open, losing Information, needing to know exact words to find things, etc...

Beyond mitigating our current problems, annotation can take the web to awesome new heights. In the #overweb, the webpage becomes the contextual footprint for intertwingled worlds of information, interactions, transactions, and experiences that emanate from ideas on the page. The resulting digital mirror world over every webpage will ultimately connect to relevant aspects of physical reality and virtual worlds.
